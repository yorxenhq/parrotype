## how it works

No windows to click, nothing steals focus. A small pill above the taskbar shows what's happening — that's the whole UI.

01

Hold Ctrl + Alt

Push-to-talk, anywhere in Windows. Prefer a toggle? Ctrl+Shift+Space starts and stops.

02

Speak

A live waveform shows the mic is hearing you. Esc cancels — nothing gets inserted.

03

Release — text lands

Whisper transcribes locally in ~0.8s and the text is typed right where your cursor is. Clipboard untouched.

## private by design

Not "privacy-friendly". Offline, verifiably.

  • Recognition runs on your machine. OpenAI Whisper via faster-whisper (CTranslate2). Your voice and text never leave it.
  • The core contains no network code. Don't trust us — grep the source.
  • Two network calls, ever: the one-time model download at setup, and an optional weekly update check you can switch off.
$ grep -rE "requests|urllib|socket" core/
# no matches — that's the point

## features

Types directly

Text is typed into any app as if from the keyboard — your clipboard stays yours.

14 languages, tested

EN, RU, ES, DE, FR, IT, PT, PL, UK, NL, TR, JA, KO, ZH — each passed our own accuracy test. Auto-detect for 90+.

Pick your model

large-v3-turbo on GPU, small on CPU by default — or any of tiny to large-v3.

GPU or CPU

NVIDIA acceleration when you have it; runs fine on CPU when you don't.

Your dictionary

Replacement rules for your names, terms and jargon — fixed before the text lands.

No accounts, no tiers, no trial. Open source you can read, fork and ship.
